We are organizing the second Familymaker Awards to honor and acknowledge the work of homemakers. Who exactly is a homemaker, is one is the biggest question? We all start and go back to a Home at the end of the day and make one, then it makes all of us a homemaker. But one person ends up taking more accountability and responsibility of running a household. The person plays the role of a mother (can be played by any gender). 

 Families are nurtured in a Home and it takes loads of work to make a house into a Home. Homemaker is not gender specific and as a family it gets to be the onus of each one to contribute to make one. However, there are multiple dimensions working simultaneously when children or elderly are involved.

There are many aspects involved in running a household, many of them fall under the category of chores (cooking, cleaning etc.) and many in relationships (parenting, caregiving, couple relationships etc.). The responsibility of various tasks might be shared but the accountability or onus lies with one person as when something goes right or wrong after the task, one person is held accountable. The person who holds the accountability of running a household is the primary Familymaker.   

As each one of us starts from a Home, skill building and inculcation of values starts from a Home. Leadership, EQ, teamwork, cooperation, patience, tolerance and many other skills are learned in a home living with people. Our endeavor is to propagate the family values which increasingly are getting lost due to the rapid commercialization of living. The society accepts work as work only when done for money and nobody does anything for money in a Home. The significance of work in a home is lost when it lacks its due respect. These awards are to regain the respect for the work done in a home. Work can be done by domestic helps but majority of the population cannot afford and do the work themselves which takes time and efforts.
